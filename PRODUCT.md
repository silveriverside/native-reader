# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Native Reader serves cross-language readers who capture book pages with a phone, then study the original text, translations, vocabulary, annotations, and notes in one local-first reading workflow. Users are typically reading printed books or scanned pages, switching between focused reading, translation lookup, vocabulary review, and later export.

## Product Purpose

The product helps users turn photographed book pages into a structured reading and learning workspace. Success means a user can capture pages, read original or translated content, review vocabulary, manage books, and preserve annotations and notes in formats that remain portable over time.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, focused, efficient, supportive, and learning-oriented. The interface should feel calm during reading, direct during management tasks, and encouraging without becoming playful or noisy.

## Anti-references

Avoid decorative study-app clutter, gamified streak-heavy dashboards, saturated inactive states, dense enterprise admin styling, and any interface pattern that distracts from long-form reading. Do not hide core book actions behind vague labels or treat error recovery as normal success.

## Design Principles

- Keep reading primary: management tools should support the reading flow rather than dominate it.
- Preserve user work: notes, annotations, metadata, and exports must feel durable and understandable.
- Make state visible: progress, pinned books, archived books, metadata completeness, and cached translation status should be clear at a glance.
- Prefer calm efficiency: use restrained surfaces, clear hierarchy, and direct action labels.
- Treat failures as fixable bugs: retries and temporary fallbacks may exist, but they should communicate that something needs attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Support dynamic font sizes where practical, sufficient touch targets, reduced motion preferences, and color-blind-friendly state indicators that do not rely on color alone.
